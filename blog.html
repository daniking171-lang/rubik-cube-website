Introduction


The Rubic Cube is a 3D combination puzzle invented in

1974 by Ernő Rubik. It has fascinated puzzle lovers for

decades and is a staple in the world of speedcubing.










History of Rubic Cube


Initially called the "Magic Cube," it became a worldwide

sensation in the 1980s. The puzzle consists of 6 faces with

9 stickers each, and the goal is to align colors on each

face.










How to Solve


Solving a Rubic Cube involves understanding its notation,

Tips & Tricks


Start slow, focus on one layer at a time, and memorize key

algorithms. Speedcubers often use finger tricks and

practice solving under timed conditions to improve their

speed.










Conclusion


The Rubic Cube is more than just a toy; it improves

problem-solving skills, hand-eye coordination, and logical

thinking. Mastering it is a rewarding experience for

anyone.
